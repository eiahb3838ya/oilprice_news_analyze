update 2-ftse 100 rides to 2 month high on oil strength; ryanair slumps
feb reuters london stocks climbed to their highest in more than two months on monday as gains in oil shares and consumer staples offset deep losses in miners while airline ryanair slumped after a disappointing quarterly report
the ftse index edged percent higher to its highest since extending a winning streak after the fed cautious stance last week and jobs data boosted confidence
but some gains in the index evaporated as the pound handed back initial losses while the more domestically focussed index ended with a percent gain
sterling gained after a newspaper reported that goods shipped to britain from the european union could be waved through without checks in the event of a brexit
on wall street stocks edged higher thanks to a boost from technology shares ahead of alphabet results while declines in healthcare and energy stocks weighed
in contrast british oil majors shell and bp held on to gains and provided the most support for the main bourse as crude prices earlier hit their highest so far this year on supply cuts and sanctions against venezuela petroleum industry
cyclicals such as miners and dollar earners led the falls while consumer staples such as unilever diageo which are considered safe havens in times of economic and geopolitical stress were in favour
miners broke a winning streak and suffered their worst fall in more than days as copper prices fell on concerns about slowing factory activity in china which is the world biggest industrial metals consumer
shares in ryanair shaved some of the day losses to end down percent after europe biggest budget airline blamed falling fares for its quarterly loss and warned that overcapacity was likely to continue to pressure prices
on the midcaps iron ore pellet producer ferrexpo slumped percent after it said its auditors received bank statements from a charity which was set up mainly to coordinate its corporate social responsibility programme that contained as yet unexplained discrepancies
ferrexpo was the worst faller ending the day with steepest decline in six months
in single stock moves supermarket chain morrisons advanced nearly percent to be among best ftse performers after citigroup raised the rating to neutral
meanwhile the broader picture remained bleak with multiple downbeat data from the world economy
a survey by accountants deloitte found that major british businesses appetite to take on financial risk has fallen to its lowest level in nearly a decade due to fears of the hardest of brexits and rising protectionism
growth in britain construction industry also dipped in january to its weakest in months as brexit uncertainty dragged on commercial building work another survey showed
worries about brexit were exacerbated by nissan announcement over the weekend that it had scrapped plans to build its new suv in britain and its warning that uncertainty over britain departure was making it harder to plan for the future
the nissan cancellation was a blow and reflects the underlying reality that brexit uncertainty inexorably impacts business confidence and hence related investments said raymond james analyst chris bailey
