u.s. oil prices inch up, but global economic concerns weigh
singapore dec reuters oil prices edged up on monday amid a fall in drilling activity in the united states but lingering concerns about weaker economic growth in major economies kept a lid on gains
west texas intermediate wti crude futures were at per barrel at gmt up percent or cents from their last settlement
international brent crude oil futures had yet to trade
despite opec and russia agreeing to reduce oil production by a further million barrels per day at the recent opec meeting prices have fallen as the market awaits evidence that the cuts will balance the market anz bank said on monday
even a fall in the rig count could dispel these concerns
drillers cut four oil rigs in the week to pulling the total count to the lowest since at general electric co baker hughes energy services firm said on friday
the organisation of the petroleum exporting countries and its allies have agreed to curb output from january by million barrels per day bpd while allies contribute an additional million bpd of cuts in a move to be reviewed at a meeting in april
oil markets have been struggling for direction with traders reading between the headlines and watching inventory numbers to gauge shale output said stephen innes head of trading for at futures brokerage oanda in singapore
the more opec tries to cut supply and drive prices higher the bigger the door opens for shale producers
growing concerns about weakening growth in major economies such as china and europe also dampened the mood in markets for oil and other asset classes
chinese oil refinery throughput in november fell from october suggesting an easing in oil demand while the country industrial output rose the least in nearly three years as the economy continued to lose momentum
meanwhile french business activity plunged unexpectedly into contraction this month retreating at the fastest pace in over four years while germany private sector expansion slowed to a low in december
reporting by koustav samanta in singapore editing by joseph radford
