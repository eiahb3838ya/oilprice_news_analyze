drillers add rigs drilling for new oil graphic on vs china tariffs by henning gloystein singapore april reuters oil markets stabilised on monday after slumping around percent last friday on the back of concerns of an intensifying trade dispute between the united states and china as well as increased drilling activity
wti crude futures were at a barrel at gmt up cents or percent from their previous settlement
brent crude futures were at per barrel up cents or percent
oil prices fell about percent on friday after president donald trump threatened new tariffs on china reigniting fears of a trade war between the world s two largest economies that could hurt global growth
while it s possible an escalating trade war could dent global growth sentiment the real fear is that china if pushed hard enough could slap a tax on oil imported into china said stephen innes head of trading for at futures brokerage oanda in singapore
in physical oil markets opec s number two producer iraq said on monday that it is keeping prices for its crude supplies in may steady
in the united states drillers added rigs looking for new production in the week to april bringing the total count to the highest level since march general electric s baker hughes energy services firm said on friday
as a result exports have soared in recent months more than offsetting the venezuelan supply disruption as a result of the economic crisis in the south american innes said
despite this oil prices have generally been supported by healthy demand as well as by supply restraint led by the organization of the petroleum exporting countries opec which started in in order to rein in oversupply and prop up prices
