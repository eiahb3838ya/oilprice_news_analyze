update 1-oklahoma orders cut to disposal well volumes following quakes
houston reuters oklahoma regulators forced an oil and gas producer to reduce operations on a well used for disposing saltwater following a large earthquake over the weekend that set off a series of seismic activity in the state matt skinner spokesman for the oklahoma corporation commission occ said on monday
the temblors occurred near perry oklahoma in the northwestern part of the state within an area singled out by regulators for the frequency of earthquakes from oil and gas production activities
the first quake occurred on saturday registering at magnitude and was followed by several others including a magnitude earthquake that hit near perry early monday morning according to the geological survey
the occ forced m m energy inc to reduce operations on saturday on a disposal well in the area from barrels per day bpd to bpd skinner said
this is an ongoing situation and further actions are possible the occ wrote in a notice issued midday monday
a representative for m m energy did not immediately respond to a request for comment
the reduction will be phased in with the final volume reached no later than days skinner added
it was unclear whether the directive had impacted production
disposal wells have been blamed for an uptick in earthquakes across the state in recent years which topped out at magnitude or greater earthquakes in versus just quakes per year in according to the oklahoma geological survey
the oklahoma corporation commission has since issued tougher regulations on oil and gas operators in affected areas in a bid to curb the quakes
the frequency of magnitude or greater earthquakes fell to just over last year
perry oklahoma is about miles from cushing a town sometimes referred to as the pipeline crossroads of the world and home to massive storage tanks
currently about million barrels of oil are held in cushing according to the energy information administration
a spokesman for magellan midstream partners which owns about million barrels of capacity at cushing said its pipelines and terminals were operating normally on monday
enbridge energy partners which has about million barrels of storage also said there was no impact to its operations and blueknight energy partners said operations were normal
phillips said its ponca city refinery about miles from perry was not affected
