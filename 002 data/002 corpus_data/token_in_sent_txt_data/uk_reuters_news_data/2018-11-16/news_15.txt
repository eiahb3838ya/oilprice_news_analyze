iraq restarts small kirkuk oil exports after a year of suspension
london nov reuters iraq has restarted exports of kirkuk oil interrupted more than a year ago amid a standoff between the central government in baghdad and kurdistan s region industry sources said on friday
the development is a win for the government which has been putting pressure on both sides to settle the dispute and resume flows to help address a shortage of iranian crude in the region after washington imposed new sanctions on tehran
the flows resumed on friday at a modest level of around barrels per day versus peak levels of observed during some months of and it was not clear when and by how much they will rise sources said
