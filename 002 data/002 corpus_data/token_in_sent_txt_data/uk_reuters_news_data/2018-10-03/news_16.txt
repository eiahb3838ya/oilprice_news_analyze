update 1-india allows oil firms hit by rising costs to raise $10 bln overseas
adds details by sanjeev miglani and nidhi verma oct reuters india said on wednesday it will allow state oil marketing firms to raise billion in overseas loans to help them deal with a sharp rise in crude oil prices and a falling rupee currency
the reserve bank of india said it will relax the external commercial borrowings ecb policy to allow the oil companies to raise external debt for working capital purposes and lifted the individual borrowing limit which was set at million
india the world s third biggest oil importer depends on overseas markets to meet percent of its oil needs
economic affairs secretary subhash chandra garg said the moves will help the oil companies to raise as much as billion for maturity periods of three to five years
the rupee has lost as much as percent to the dollar since the beginning of the year adding to the nation s oil import bill at a time when crude is hovering at around a barrel
state oil marketing companies have been tapping local markets to raise funds to meet their working capital needs mainly to pay for oil imports billed in dollars
this in turn has pushed up demand for dollars in the local market piling the pressure on falling rupee
india also waived a requirement for the companies to hedge dollars while raising funds from the overseas markets
