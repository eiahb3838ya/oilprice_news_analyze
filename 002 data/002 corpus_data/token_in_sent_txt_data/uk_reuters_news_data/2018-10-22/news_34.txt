oil holds steady as rising u.s. rig count offsets iran sanctions worries
sanctions against iran oil exports to start rig count at highest since trade war clouds outlook by henning gloystein singapore oct reuters oil prices were stable on monday supported by supply concerns ahead of the start of sanctions against iran s crude exports but held back by rising drilling activity in the united states
brent crude oil futures were trading at a barrel at gmt cents below their last close at the end of last week
west texas intermediate wti crude futures were at a barrel cents below their last settlement
sanctions against iran s oil exports will start on november
while the organization of the petroleum exporting countries opec agreed in june to boost supply to make up for expected iran disruptions an internal document reviewed by reuters suggested that opec is struggling to add barrels to the market as an increase in saudi arabian supply was offset by declines in iran venezuela and angola
traders said major oil consumers were stockpiling in anticipation of more disruptions
in china higher seasonal demand and suspected stockpiling are occurring while similarly the and the oecd continue building stockpiles ahead of potential supply disruptions this winter said stephen innes head of trading for at futures brokerage oanda in singapore
despite this innes said overall global oil supply was currently enough to meet demand
while the equilibrium remains fragile brent remains in good supply despite growing uncertainty about supply disruptions he said
there were also some signs of rising output especially in north america
drillers added four oil rigs in the week to bringing the total count to baker hughes energy services firm said on friday raising the rig count to the highest level since march
the rig count is an early indicator of future output
with activity rising again after months of stagnation crude production is also expected to continue to rise
looking further out concern that the trade dispute between the united states and china would crimp economic growth may weigh on the outlook for oil prices
the full impact of the trade war will hit markets in and could act as a considerable drag on oil demand next year raising the possibility of the market returning to surplus said emirates nbd bank in a note
