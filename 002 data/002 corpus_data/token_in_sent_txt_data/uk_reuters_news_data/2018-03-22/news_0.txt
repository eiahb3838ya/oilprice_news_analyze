ex-cameroon minister arrested in nigeria, extradited home
yaounde march reuters cameroon s former water and energy minister was arrested in nigeria and flown home on thursday a police source and local media said amid a crackdown on corruption
basile atangana kouna was removed from his ministerial position during president paul biya s march cabinet reshuffle
he had already been ordered the previous month not to leave the country
atangana kouna is in cameroon
he was arrested in nigeria and sent back to cameroon
he arrived this evening one police source who asked not to be named as he was not authorised to speak to the press told reuters
vision a private television channel considered allied to biya reported that the former minister arrived at the international airport in the capital yaounde at pm gmt escorted by police officers
another channel canal international also reported his arrest in nigeria and subsequent extradition back to cameroon
cameroon s government spokesman was not immediately reachable for comment
neither were officials in neighbouring nigeria
it was not clear what charges atangana kouna was facing but there was speculation on thursday that he was arrested as part of an drive known as operation sparrowhawk
three men a former ministry of public works official the head of a state company and the of the university of douala were arrested as part of the operation earlier this week
cameroon s oil wealth has spawned a sprawling political patronage system fueled by revenues from crude exports
transparency international ranked it out of countries on its annual corruption perceptions index
biya has ruled virtually by decree since taking over from a retiring predecessor in and then winning an election with percent of the vote a year later
now biya is expected to again seek in presidential polls later this year but he is currently facing some of the biggest challenges to his rule
cameroon s economy has been crippled by low crude prices
and separatists are mounting a guerrilla campaign in the country s two provinces demanding independence from the majority francophone central african nation
