nepal's new prime minister names former c.bank chief as finance minister
kathmandu feb reuters nepal s prime minister on monday named former central banker yubaraj khatiwada as the himalayan nation s finance minister to revive a economy emerging from a long civil conflict

sharma oli who was appointed for a second term last week campaigned on a promise to fight corruption and boost growth
analysts say the nearly victory his coalition with maoist former rebels won in the parliament gives him the mandate to usher in change
khatiwada who had served as governor of the central bank nepal rastra bank is a monetary policy expert and an alumnus of the delhi school of economics in neighbouring india
new ministers in oli s core team were also sworn in on monday a presidential spokesman said
oli named ram bahadur thapa a former guerrilla commander of the maoist party as the interior minister in charge of law and order and national administration
an alliance between oli and former rebel chief prachanda swept to victory in elections late last year
the maoists ended their civil war under a peace deal
his nationalism card was a useful tool for elections but now he has to deliver on his campaign promise of stability and prosperity said kunda dixit editor of the nepali times weekly
nepal has seen changes of government in as many years
instability has fed corruption slowed growth and hit recovery from a earthquake that killed people
western diplomats say the stunning election win by the left emboldened nepal s other giant neighbour china which is using its belt and road initiative bri to strengthen ties
oli took a tough position with new delhi during a border blockade in and reaching out to china for the use of its roads and ports potentially ending his landlocked nation s sole dependence on india for transit
he has vowed to revoke a decision by the previous government to scrap a deal with china gezhouba group corp to build the budhi gandaki power plant near kathmandu
both asian giants compete to influence nepal home to mount everest the world s highest mountain and have injected huge amounts of money in aid and investment
