update 9-oil steadies as saudi tensions balance demand outlook
new york reuters oil prices steadied on monday supported by geopolitical tension over the disappearance of a saudi journalist that has stoked worries about supplies from riyadh but weighed by concern over demand outlook
brent crude futures for december delivery rose cents to settle at a barrel
west texas intermediate wti crude futures rose cents to settle at a barrel
last week both contracts fell by more than percent as stock markets tumbled
however rising geopolitical tension between the united states the world s top oil consumer and saudi arabia one of the biggest crude producers supported prices on monday
riyadh has been under pressure since journalist jamal khashoggi a critic of the kingdom and a resident disappeared on after visiting the saudi consulate in istanbul
president donald trump has threatened severe punishment if it is found that khashoggi was killed in the consulate
saudi arabia said it would retaliate for any action against it over the khashoggi case state news agency spa reported on sunday quoting an official source
this comes at a critical time for global oil markets which are bracing for sanctions against iran due to come into force on
the united states is still aiming to cut iran s oil sales to zero washington s special envoy for iran said on monday
turkey and italy are the last buyers of iranian crude outside china india and the middle east according to tanker data and an industry source the latest sign that shipments are taking a major hit from the looming sanctions

however some risk premium was taken out of the market when trump on monday raised the possibility that rogue killers could have been responsible for khashoggi s disappearance
exerting downward pressure on prices friday s monthly report from the international energy agency said the market looked adequately supplied for now and cut its forecasts for world oil demand growth this year and next
the secretary general of the organization of the petroleum exporting countries last week said that the group saw the oil market as well supplied and that it was wary of creating a glut next year
market participants also focused on a weakening gasoline crack spread
