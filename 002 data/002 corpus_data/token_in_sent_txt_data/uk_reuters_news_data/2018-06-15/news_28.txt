update 3-india's top bank to stop handling iran oil payments, imports may be hit
iran was india s third biggest oil supplier reimposes sanctions after quitting iran deal sbi wants to protect operations source adds comments from sbi source bpcl by nidhi verma new delhi june reuters india s imports of iranian oil may be hit from the end of august after the state bank of india informed refiners it would not handle payments for the crude from november the finance chief of indian oil corp ioc said on friday
the move by the bank india s biggest comes after president donald trump pulled out of an international nuclear deal with iran last month pledging to reimpose tough sanctions within days
oil loading will be affected from under the current mechanism unless a new payment route is established ioc s sharma told reuters in a telephone interview on friday
some sanctions take effect after a period ending on and the rest notably affecting the petroleum sector after a period ending on
although new delhi had cut imports from tehran in due to a dispute over a giant gas field iran remained its oil supplier
iran supplied about barrels per day bpd or about a of india s more than million bpd of imports in the fiscal year to march
sbi has written to the indian refiners and the government that it would not be able to handle oil payments to iran from nov an official at sbi said
we have said that we can not handle oil payments to iran in any currency as that will have an immediate impact on our operations this source said
an sbi spokesman did not immediately respond to requests for comment
india s refiners currently use sbi and handelsbank ag eih to buy iranian oil in euros according to ioc and other companies
india iran s top oil client after china was one of the few nations that continued to trade with tehran during a previous round of western sanctions
the south asian nation has said it does not follow sanctions but companies and banks with links to the financial system could face penalties if they do not comply
iran offers indian refiners a credit period on oil sales which means payment for cargoes loaded from the end of august will be due in november
analysts feel iran may have to offer more incentives to protect its oil sales to india
indians may ask iran for more discount in exchange for early payment for oil purchases instead of a credit period said sri paravaikkarasu head of east of suez oil at consultancy fge
seeking to safeguard oil imports from iran and scout for alternate payment avenues an indian delegation last week visited officials and bankers in france germany britain and brussels the base for the european union
that visit was only exploratory in nature we are still watching the situation indian oil minister dharmendra pradhan told reuters this week
apart from banks shipping firms and insurance companies have already begun distancing themselves from iran
ioc s sharma said his firm received very few responses to inquiries on hiring tankers to import iranian oil
only nitc national iranian oil company and a few others participated he said adding freight rates for transportation of iranian oil have also gone up due to the threat of sanctions
ioc previously said it would look at buying oil from traditional suppliers mostly in the middle east to compensate for any cut in supplies from iran related to sanctions
indian previously drew up plans to almost double oil imports from iran which had offered deep discounts on shipping of crude
officials at other state refiners hindustan petroleum and bharat petroleum corp confirmed receiving the sbi letter
refiner reliance industries ltd owner of the world s biggest refining complex plans to halt oil imports from iran while nayara energy has started cutting purchases from this month sources previously told reuters
bpcl s head of refiners ramachandran said there were other potential suppliers including canada and the united states or other middle east states that cold replace iranian crude
we hope suppliers like iraq and saudi arabia will fill the gap he said
we will get alternatives similar to iranian oil but we will have to pay extra
