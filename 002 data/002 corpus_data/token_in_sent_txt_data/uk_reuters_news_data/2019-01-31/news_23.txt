india's top gas importer won't bid for bangladesh's planned lng terminal
new delhi jan reuters india s top gas importer petronet lng will not bid for the proposed million tonnes per annum mtpa liquefied natural gas terminal in bangladesh and instead wants a deal its managing director prabhat singh said on thursday
singh said he was upbeat about the quarter earnings of his firm
petronet lng hopes spot lng prices will go down
the company is planning to bid for an lng terminal in andaman
petronet lng is keen to set up a mtpa lng terminal on india s east coast and that could cost up to billion rupees million singh said
the company is also looking at buying a stake in indian oil corp s ennore lng terminal
petronet hopes to complete the expansion of dahej lng terminal to mtpa by april
