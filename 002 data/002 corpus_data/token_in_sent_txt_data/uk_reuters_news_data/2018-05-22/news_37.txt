china's henan shenhuo to shift aluminium smelting capacity to yunnan province
to transfer tonnes per year of capacity from henan joins others in courting yunnan s hydropower potential construction of project to start in july by tom daly beijing may reuters china s henan shenhuo coal power co will transfer more than tonnes per year of aluminium smelting capacity to the southwestern province of yunnan according to a provincial government notice
the move illustrates yunnan s increasing popularity as a location for aluminium smelters in china the world s biggest producer of the metal
the companies are keen to tap the province s large hydropower capacity to replace their current plants for the power needed during the smelting process
the yunnan industry and information technology commission said late on monday that yunnan shenhuo aluminium co a unit of shenhuo had completed the formalities to transfer tonnes per year of smelting capacity to the province
this represents the first phase of a tonnes per year integrated hydropower and aluminium smelting project shenhuo is building in yunnan it said
construction is expected to start on july with production to begin in a year
china s ministry of industry and information technology allows smelters to move or acquire new capacity in regions that have available quotas
yunnan is a hot spot said jackie wang an aluminium analyst at consultants cru in beijing noting that another chinese smelter sichuan qiya had recently decided to relocate tonnes per year of capacity to the province
the yunnan industry and information technology commission announced that transfer on may saying the company was having trouble building a planned project with the same capacity in the xinjiang region in northwest china
yunnan s hydropower provides an alternative to power generation and that will help smelters produce green metal said wang
aluminum corp of china or chinalco is planning to link up with existing companies in yunnan
shenhuo coal power could not immediately be reached for comment
the tonnes of capacity in yunnan is replacing a total of tonnes at three plants in the central chinese province of henan that the group has already closed down
the first of these a facility stopped production in and was demolished in while the last a plant was only closed last month and is to be demolished in december
