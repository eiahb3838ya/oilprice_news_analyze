update 1-austria's omv stands by iran project but no investment yet
vienna reuters austrian energy group omv is continuing with planned iranian energy projects despite the united states withdrawal from a nuclear pact with tehran but said on tuesday it had made no investments there yet
european firms doing business in iran face sanctions after president donald trump withdrew from a deal between the france germany britain russia china and iran which lifted sanctions in exchange for tehran limiting its nuclear programme
the other signatories have indicated that they hope to salvage the deal
french finance minister bruno le maire brought up the idea that the european union could compensate european companies that might face sanctions
last week french energy giant total joined other european companies in signalling a possible exit from iran in light of trump s decision
omv was monitoring political developments in the and the european union very closely omv s upstream chief johann pleininger told its annual shareholder meeting in vienna
the project has not come to a standstill it is continuing pleininger said with regard to iran adding that no investments have been made yet
the austrian group which generates the bulk of its profit in europe started operations in iran in as the operator of the mehr exploration block in the west of the country
omv halted operations in due to sanctions but following sanctions relief it signed in may a memorandum with the national iranian oil company nioc for projects located in the zagros area in the west and the fars field in the south where foreign firms often need a local partner
in june omv and russia s gazprom neft announced a memorandum of understanding to work in iran s oil sector
at last year s shareholders meeting pleininger said the nioc still owed omv million million pounds
omv bought iranian crude oil in april a spokesman said this month but did give any more detail
omv is austria s former state petroleum company and the government still holds a percent stake
its investor is abu dhabi industrial group mubadala investment with percent
