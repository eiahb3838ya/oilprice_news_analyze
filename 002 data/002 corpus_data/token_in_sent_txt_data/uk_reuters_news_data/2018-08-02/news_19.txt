south africa's solidarity union accepts latest eskom wage offer
johannesburg aug reuters south africa s solidarity trade union said on thursday it had accepted the latest wage offer from struggling power firm eskom but the other two bigger unions demanding pay hikes were yet to respond
eskom is on a drive as it tries to emerge from a financial crisis and has been grappling with labour unrest for weeks after it initially refused to raise salaries
it caved in to union demands for higher pay after protesting workers forced some of its generating units to be switched off leading to power outages in africa s most industrialised economy
solidarity accepted a salary increase of percent this year and percent next year and the year after plus an increase in housing allowances and a cash payment of rand the union which represents mostly skilled workers said in a statement
for all our members across south africa the right thing to do now would be to help prevent the economy from being hampered by an unreliable power supply solidarity chief executive dirk hermann said
the national union of mineworkers and national union of metalworkers of south africa two larger unions at eskom are still discussing eskom s latest offer with their members
eskom met union leaders on thursday and said it hoped to find a solution to the wage impasse
repairing eskom s depleted balance sheet is a major challenge for south african president cyril ramaphosa
sources in the ruling african national congress have said eskom assets may be sold but this would likely face union opposition
