west virginia allows etp to resume work on rover natgas pipeline
may reuters environmental regulators in west virginia said energy transfer partners lp could restart work on its billion rover natural gas pipeline after ordering the company to stop some work in the state due to permit violations the west virginia department of environmental protection dep said last week it lifted the march cease and desist order on the rover sherwood lateral on may
rover is the biggest gas pipe under construction in the united states
it is designed to carry up to billion cubic feet per day bcfd of gas from the marcellus and utica shale fields in pennsylvania ohio and west virginia to the midwest and canada s ontario province
one billion cubic feet of gas is enough to fuel about million homes for a day
the latest cease and desist order was not the first time west virginia stopped etp from work on rover
the dep also ordered the company to halt some work from july to due to violations of storm water rules
in addition the federal energy regulatory commission ferc stopped etp from horizontal drilling in ohio from may to after an estimated million gallons of drilling fluid spilled into a wetland near the tuscarawas river
energy companies use horizontal drilling to cross under obstacles such as rivers and highways
before those stop work orders etp expected to finish rover in november
etp said it still expects to complete the project by the end of the second quarter of
rover has been partially in service since august and is now able to transport up to bcfd
major producers signed up to use rover include units of privately held ascent resources antero resources corp range resources corp southwestern energy co eclipse resources corp and eqt corp
