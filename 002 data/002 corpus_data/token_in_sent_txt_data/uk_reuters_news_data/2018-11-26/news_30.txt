germany's scholz against compensation for companies hit by coal exit - sources
berlin nov reuters german finance minister olaf scholz has shown little sympathy to pay compensation for operators of power plants that could be forced to shut down earlier in an effort to meet ambitious climate goals by sources said on monday
scholz is also sceptical of other measures to help companies cope with the effects of the planned brown coal exit such as lowering energy tax two people familiar with the talks said
a finance ministry spokeswoman declined to comment
