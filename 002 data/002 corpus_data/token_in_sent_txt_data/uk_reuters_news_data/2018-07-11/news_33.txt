german city of stuttgart seeks to avert euro-5 diesel ban
frankfurt reuters the german city of stuttgart will bar only the most polluting diesel vehicles from its streets next year and will seek to avoid a widening of the ban by taking measures to improve air quality a state ministry said
stuttgart the heartland of germany s automotive industry plans to start banning diesel vehicles meeting the or older emissions standards from january the state ministry of said on wednesday
we want to avoid bans on diesels with a comprehensive package of clean air measures said andreas schwarz leader of the greens parliamentary group in the regional assembly
but he also said the city s measures such as an expansion of public transportation and electric mobility would need to have a sufficient effect on pollution levels
there has been a global backlash against cars since volkswagen admitted in to cheating exhaust tests meant to limit emissions of particulate matter and nitrogen oxide nox
dozens of german cities including hamburg munich and stuttgart exceed european union limits on nox which is known to cause respiratory diseases
if by it does not look like the measures stuttgart has taken to improve air quality will bring its nox levels within the legal limits the ban could be widened to diesels in january the ministry said
germany s top administrative court ruled in february in favor of allowing major cities to ban heavily polluting diesel cars
soon after hamburg became the first german city to ban diesel cars that do not meet the latest standards from some busy streets seeking to raise pressure on carmakers to undertake costly retrofits
a regional court in stuttgart had demanded last month the state of home to carmakers and vw unit porsche draw up a firm plan for when it will ban diesels
bans on diesel vehicles from city centers are also planned in paris madrid mexico city and athens by while the mayor of copenhagen wants to bar new diesel cars from entering the city center as soon as next year
