mideast stocks-most gulf markets weak as saudi opens higher on oil price rise
dubai sept reuters saudi arabia s stock market opened higher on wednesday as oil prices rose and the global reaction to the latest escalation in the united trade dispute remained relatively limited
oil edged up on concerns that producers may not be able to cover a shortfall in supply once sanctions on iran come into effect despite gains in stockpiles
saudi arabia s main index was up in morning trade as petrochemical company saudi kayan jumped percent and bank added percent
saudi basic industries corp sabic extended tuesday s gains to be up percent about one hour after the market opened
sabic the world s number four chemical firm announced with switzerland s clariant on tuesday that they would merge their materials businesses
sabic bought a percent stake in clariant earlier this year
saudi s united electronics was up percent after a shareholders meeting approved a capital increase via a bonus share issue
elsewhere in the gulf most markets were trading lower
in abu dhabi telecommunications group etisalat was down percent and union national bank which is in merger talks with other abu dhabi banks was percent lower
the index was down percent
qatar s index was weighed down by banking shares as masraf al rayan dropped percent and commercial bank fell percent in morning trade
the index was down percent
the dubai index was trading down percent with dubai islamic bank dropping percent and major developer damac properties falling percent
