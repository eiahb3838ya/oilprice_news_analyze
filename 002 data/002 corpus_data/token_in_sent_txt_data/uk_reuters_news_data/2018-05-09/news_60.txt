below is a list of deals and memoranda of understanding mou that iran has signed with foreign companies which could now be under threat amid speculation washington could impose secondary sanctions on firms that continue to work with tehran
total became in november the first oil major to sign a big deal with tehran since it was to international trade markets agreeing to help it develop the world s largest gas field south pars
the french company said in october it would have to review the project if the united states decided to impose unilateral sanctions on tehran given the company s assets in the market
china s top oil and gas company cnpc was considering taking over total s stake in the project if the french company left industry sources said in october
norwegian oil and gas company dno said in november it was the second western energy company after total to sign a deal with iran under which it agreed to study the development of the changuleh oilfield in western iran
shell signed a provisional deal in december to develop iranian oil and gas fields south azadegan yadavaran and kish
but its annual report published in showed it had bought only three cargoes of iranian oil since the easing of sanctions in a sign of the legal difficulties hampering trade
south korea s daewoo engineering and construction daewoo e c signed a mou in to carry out construction of an oil refinery in bandar jask on the southern coast of iran
norway s aker solutions signed an mou in may to modernise the iranian oil industry
austria s omv signed a mou in may for projects in the zagros area of western iran and the fars field in the south
in june omv and russia s gazprom neft announced a mou to work in iran s oil sector
italy s saipem signed mous in to cooperate on iranian pipeline projects upgrading of refineries and development of the tous gas field in the northeastern province of khorasan razavi
basf s wintershall oil and gas exploration arm signed a mou with the national iranian oil company in april

in january iran named companies from more than a dozen countries as being allowed to bid for oil and gas projects using a new less restrictive contract model
the firms included shell total italy s eni malaysia s petronas and russia s gazprom and lukoil as well as companies from china austria japan and elsewhere
italy s eni signed an agreement with iran in june for feasibility studies to develop an oil field and a gas field
india s oil and natural gas corp ltd in late february signed an initial agreement with an iranian company to develop the susangerd oilfield in the south of iran a company source said

norway s saga energy signed a billion euro billion deal in october to build solar power plants in iran
planemaker boeing agreed in december to sell aircraft including boeing jets to iranair

european planemaker airbus said in december it had sealed a deal to sell jets to iranair
