update 3-marathon texas city, texas refineries restoring production: sources
adds marathon comment houston may reuters marathon petroleum corp was restarting the bpd texas city texas refinery on tuesday night after a substation fire knocked out power said sources familiar with plant operations
marathon was also restarting normal production at the neighboring bpd galveston bay refinery in texas city the sources said
a bpd resid hydrotreater was restarting at the big refinery
a late afternoon fire on a centerpoint energy substation near bacliff texas miles km north of the refineries also disrupted production at valero energy corp s bpd refinery in texas city the sources said
marathon spokesman chuck rice said in a statement that the company was evaluating operations at the galveston bay refinery after a power dip in the texas city area
even though we have a cogeneration facility for the galveston bay refinery a larger power dip can affect our operations rice said
there were no evacuations or cogeneration plants produce electrical power and steam for refineries
the city of texas city emergency management office said refineries on the south side of texas city were using safety flares as they worked to restore power on tuesday
a valero spokeswoman did not reply to messages asking about operations at the texas city refinery on tuesday night
the valero refinery s unit was shut on april following an explosion and fire on the alkylation unit at the refinery the first of four fires at refineries and chemical plants across the united states in the past weeks
the tuesday fire broke out on the bacliff substation at about cdt gmt with flaring seen at the marathon and valero refineries about minutes later according energy industry intelligence service genscape
at about centerpoint said the fire which was fueled by mineral oil inside the substation was under control
all personnel at the substation were accounted for and no injuries were reported according to centerpoint
