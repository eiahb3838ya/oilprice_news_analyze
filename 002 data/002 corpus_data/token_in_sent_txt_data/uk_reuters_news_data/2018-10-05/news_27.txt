kenya on course to develop nuclear energy, official says
nairobi oct reuters kenya s ambition of having a nuclear power plant is still on course and it hopes to build its first such facility in the next years a senior energy ministry official said on friday
joseph njoroge principal secretary for electricity told reuters the east african nation plans to turn to nuclear when it has fully exploited other sources of energy
it nuclear plant construction may be in the next or even years the kenya nuclear electricity board is still a going concern and only working to prepare for nuclear he said
hydropower accounts for percent of kenya s electricity generation with the rest coming from geothermal wind and heavy oil plants the ministry says
plans to develop a plant on the coast using funding from china have been delayed by court action from environmental activists
the development of nuclear energy will come after other resources have been fully exploited in line with growing demand for energy njoroge said
that is after we have done a lot of exploitation of the geothermal the coal that we also want to exploit the wind solar and all those he said
