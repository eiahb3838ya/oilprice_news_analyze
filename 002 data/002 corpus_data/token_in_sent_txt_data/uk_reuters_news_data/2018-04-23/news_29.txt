armenian pm resigns after days of street protests
yerevan april reuters armenian prime minister serzh sarksyan said on monday he would resign to help maintain peace in the republic following daily street protests since before he took up the post on april
sarksyan a close ally of russian president vladimir putin had served as armenia s president for a decade until earlier this month and had faced accusations of clinging to power when parliament voted for him to take up the post of prime minister
earlier on monday pressure on the to quit increased sharply when unarmed armenian soldiers joined the protests in the capital yerevan which first began on april
i got it wrong sarksyan said in a statement issued by his office
in the current situation there are several solutions but i won t choose any of them
it s not my style
i am quitting the country s leadership and the post of prime minister of under a revised constitution the prime minister now holds most power in the impoverished southern caucasus nation while the presidency has become largely ceremonial
