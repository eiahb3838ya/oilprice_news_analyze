update 1-engie's belgium tihange 3 reactor to restart, easing power cut fears
adds details background paris dec reuters engie electrabel the belgian subsidiary of french energy group engie will restart its tihange nuclear reactor after completing repair works easing fears of winter power shortages in belgium
the company said it received clearance from belgium s afcn regulator on and the belgian reactor was now expected back on the electricity grid by january nearly two months ahead of the initial start date of march
the restart of tihange could ease belgium s power supply worries after six of the country s seven nuclear reactors were forced offline for prolonged repairs due to concrete degradation and maintenance outages in september
fears of winter power shortages and blackouts sent wholesale belgian power prices to record highs
nuclear power from the seven reactors operated by engie accounts for around percent of belgian power needs
engie said in a statement that with the restart of tihange in addition to three other reactors around megawatts of nuclear capacity would be available in belgium during winter
it also said that it had arranged around mw of additional capacity through a plant mobile diesel generators curbs on industrial power use and imports from neighbouring countries which will see it through winter
