iraq declares curfew, recount in kirkuk after 'illogical' vote count
kirkuk iraq may reuters the governor of iraq s kirkuk province declared a curfew on saturday and ordered a manual recount of votes there in the national election saying an electronic counting system had produced an illogical result
rakan governor of the northern region announced a curfew from midnight until gmt to prevent any ethnic or sectarian tension between its kurdish arab and ethnic turkmen communities
did not elaborate in his statement on the problem with the system
in october iraqi forces backed by shi ite militias dislodged kurdish peshmerga fighters who had taken control of kirkuk city in preventing its capture by islamic state militants who had overrun iraqi army positions in northern and western iraq
the return of the iraqi army to kirkuk was greeted with relief by the arab and turkmen populations there
saturday s elections are the first in iraq since the defeat of islamic state last year by iraqi forces backed by a coalition
