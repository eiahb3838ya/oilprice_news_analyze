rusal sanctions seen risk to kazakh coal venture -samruk energy
almaty april reuters the bogatyr komir coal mining joint venture between kazakhstan s samruk energy and rusal could face risks linked to sanctions against the russian aluminium producer samruk said on tuesday
bogatyr komir produces million tonnes of coal a year which it sells in kazakhstan and russia mostly to power plants
sanctions could hamper the venture s equipment upgrade project samruk a unit of sovereign fund samruk kazyna said in a written reply to questions from reuters
probable risks have been identified including with regards to bogatyr komir in which rusal indirectly owns a percent stake the firm said
samruk said it had contacted thyssenkrupp a supplier of equipment for the venture and the eurasian development bank asking them to confirm whether they would continue working with bogatyr komir
it provided no other details
