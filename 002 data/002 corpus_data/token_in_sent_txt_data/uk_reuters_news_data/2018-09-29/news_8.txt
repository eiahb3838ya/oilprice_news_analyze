update 2-china hikes 2019 non-state oil import quota as private refiners expand
adding comment background beijing sept reuters china has hiked its crude oil import quota for trade generally meaning independent refiners by percent to million tonnes as two private companies prepare to launch commercial production at major new plants
it is the second consecutive year that beijing has increased the quota which is equivalent to million barrels per day bpd
the commerce ministry said in a statement companies must submit their applications for the quotas by
the ministry did not provide a detailed breakdown of quota recipients but they should include mostly independent refiners which make up around of the total
the main reason is that some major independent refineries have new production capacity that will go online in said dong xiucheng a professor at the china petroleum university
at least two private refiners hengli group and zhejiang petrochemical are preparing to launch commercial production of two new large plants
the move also underscores the growth of large private refiners which are looking to build new petrochemcial plants making plastics rubber and polyester as consumers demand goods from cars to electronics
the government has cracked down on smaller operators often known as teapots which often use outdated equipment to make lower grade fuels
