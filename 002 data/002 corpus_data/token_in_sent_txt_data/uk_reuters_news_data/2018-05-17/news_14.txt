colombia evacuates nearly 5,000 people amid fears dam may burst
bogota reuters colombia has ordered the evacuation of nearly people living along the cauca river in the northern part of the country after construction problems at a hydroelectric dam prompted fears of massive flooding
heavy rains have increased water levels in the cauca which feeds the ituango dam in antioquia province the country s hydroelectric project
problems with filling mechanisms and tunnels at the dam have authorities on high alert
we are working jointly with all institutions on the scenario which is the breaking of the dam which would provoke a huge flood in municipalities said jorge londono the head of empresas publicas de medellin the public utility company that owns the dam
that s a catastrophic scenario londono added
the dam which has not yet begun power generation has cost nearly billion to build and is meant to generate percent of colombia s electricity needs
a total of people from areas were moved to shelters away from the flood zone the andean country s disaster agency said in a statement
some people live in the towns and populated areas in antioquia bolivar cordoba and sucre provinces that could eventually be affected by possible flooding authorities said
