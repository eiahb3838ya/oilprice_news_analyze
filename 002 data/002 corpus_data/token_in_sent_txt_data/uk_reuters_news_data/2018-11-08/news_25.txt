india to allow foreign companies to store oil in padur reserve
new delhi nov reuters india on thursday approved a plan to allow foreign oil companies to store oil in the padur strategic petroleum reserve law minister prasad said as the world s third biggest oil importer seeks to cut storage costs
global oil producers including saudi arabia have shown interest in investing in india s strategic oil storage which the country uses to hedge against energy security risks as it relies heavily on oil imports
india has built emergency storage in underground caverns at three locations to hold million barrels of crude
the underground million tonne padur strategic reserve in southern karnataka state has four compartments to hold about million barrels of oil

he said india would have the first right to the oil stored in the caverns in case of emergency
