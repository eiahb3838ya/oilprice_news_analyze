sweden's vattenfall boosts its ev charging market with volvo cars deal
oslo reuters swedish utility vattenfall is partnering with volvo cars to help owners recharge their electric vehicles ev at home the firms said on thursday
the move comes two weeks after vattenfall launched plans to become northwestern europe s largest operator of infrastructure within five years

the carmaker has a target for percent of the cars it sells to be electric by volvo cars said in a joint statement with vattenfall

a functioning infrastructure for charging is therefore needed both in the community and where our customers live said kristian elvefors volvo cars managing director
about percent of all electric vehicle charging takes place at work or at home said vattenfall
under the partnership volvo car owners can visit the carmaker s website and order a charging box

others vying for a share of the charging business include french utility engie germany s innogy and chargepoint whose owners include bmw daimler and siemens
