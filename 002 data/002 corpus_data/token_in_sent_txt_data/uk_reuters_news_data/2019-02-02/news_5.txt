south africa's eff party says in election manifesto to nationalise mines
pretoria reuters south africa s radical leftist economic freedom fighters eff will nationalise all mines by if elected this year the party said in its election manifesto launched on saturday
the party is using an jobs creation and state control of the economy platform to challenge the governing african national congress anc in the election expected in may
the eff government will nationalise all mines and mineral wealth in south africa by the year
it will allocate key shares in south africa s mineral and petroleum resources to the sovereign wealth fund the party said in the manifesto adding that a state mining company would be established
since its formation in the eff which draws much of its support from the rural poor has called for the nationalisation of mines banks and other strategic sectors of the economy as well as expropriation of land without compensation
while parliament has opened the way to a change of the constitution to allow land to be expropriated without compensation a final decision is not expected until after the election
we can not postpone the land question we can not postpone the jobs question it must happen now eff leader julius malema told supporters many dressed in red and carrying placards at the manifesto launch in pretoria
land is a issue in south africa where racial inequality remains entrenched more than two decades after the end of apartheid when millions among the black majority were dispossessed of their land by a white minority
