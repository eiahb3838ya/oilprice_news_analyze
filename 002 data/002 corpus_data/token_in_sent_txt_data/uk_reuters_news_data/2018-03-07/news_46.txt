france's engie exploring sale of german power plants - sources
frankfurt reuters french energy group engie is considering selling its power plant assets in germany in a deal that could fetch a few hundred million euros two people familiar with the process told reuters
engie owns or holds stakes in three hard power plants in germany
it is the sole owner of the megawatt mw zolling site near munich and the mw farge plant and has a percent stake in the mw site in wilhelmshaven
one of the people said engie had hired jp morgan to advise on selling the assets which could also include its local retail business
the source said the sale could also cover engie s mw centrale rotterdam plant in the netherlands
engie s wilhelmshaven plant in germany is one of the most modern in europe with an efficiency level of percent
switzerland s bkw energie ag has a percent stake and municipal utility wsw energie wasser ag owns percent
in addition to capacity engie s zolling plant has two mw gas turbines that can be switched on in times of high demand or undersupply to balance out demand swings
engie also operates a mw biomass plant there
possible buyers for the assets could include czech energy group eph and german utility rwe which have both snapped up conventional power plants in the past or have announced plans to do so the people said
engie eph jp morgan and rwe all declined to comment
