maldives denies japan's allegation that it breached north korean sanctions
colombo reuters maldives on wednesday denied a japanese foreign ministry statement that said a vessel was used to illegally transfer goods from a north tanker in defiance of security council resolutions
the japanese statement said the chon ma san designated by the united states as a sanctions target was spotted by a surveillance plane with the tanker xin yuan some km miles east of shanghai on saturday
it said japan strongly suspects that the vessels conducted transfers banned by security council resolutions
maldives government denied that xin yuan is of maldivian origin and said no such vessel is registered in the we condemn the use of our national flag in a manner so as to tarnish the good standing and reputation of our nation the maldives government said in a statement
it said maldives has prioritised the implementation of all security council resolutions including those on north korea
authorities do not allow flags of convenience to foreign owned vessels to operate outside maldivian waters it said adding that the government was investigating
the maldives will pursue aggressive action against any such acts which affects the national identity in such a detrimental manner it said
the maldives is facing a political crisis that has hurt tourism its main revenue source and any international action could hurt its economy further analysts say
it is the fourth time japan has suspected such an illegal transfer in recent weeks and comes as washington and key asian allies preparer to expand the interceptions of ships suspected of violating north korean sanctions
north korea last year conducted dozens of missile launches and its sixth and largest nuclear test as it pursues its goal of developing a nuclear missile capable of reaching the united states triggering deeper security council sanctions
washington on friday slapped sanctions on dozens more companies and vessels linked to north korea s shipping trade and urged the united nations to blacklist some entities to shut down smuggling aimed at obtaining oil and selling coal
