monument-filled, traffic-clogged rome to ban diesel cars by 2024
rome reuters rome one of europe s most cities and home to thousands of ancient outdoor monuments threatened by pollution plans to ban diesel cars from the center by its mayor said
virginia raggi announced the decision on her facebook page on tuesday saying if we want to intervene seriously we have to have the courage to adopt strong measures
her comments followed a court ruling in germany that cities there can ban the most heavily polluting diesel cars from their streets
about of the million new cars sold in italy last year were diesel according to industry figures
rome has no major industries so nearly all of the air pollution in the italian capital is caused by motor vehicles
the city regularly tries to ban older more polluting vehicles from roads on days when pollution reaches critical levels
it has also tried to reduce pollution by allowing only cars whose number plates end in either odd or even numbers to circulate on alternate days
but both regulations are widely flouted and lightly enforced by traffic police
to skirt the alternate days regulation many families buy a used car with a different number plate
apart from health issues pollution from combustion engines causes severe damage to rome s many ancient outdoor monuments
according to a study last year by a branch of the culture ministry stone monuments and bronze sculptures risk serious deterioration because of air pollution
ahead of celebrations marking the start of the new millennium in the darkened facade of peter s basilica at the vatican was cleaned as part of a project that lasted several years
but fresh signs of stains are visible again
