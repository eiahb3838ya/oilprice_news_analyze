update 1-iraqi oil minister expects prices to rise over time
baghdad reuters iraqi oil minister thamer ghadhban said on monday he expected the fall in oil prices to stop and for prices to rise over time adding that if opec had not cut production prices would have dropped to per barrel
speaking at a ministry event in baghdad ghadhban said the recent fall in iraqi exports was not due to technical reasons as iraqi oil fields have high capacity but rather because of weather conditions
our goal is to reach an export capacity of million barrels per day but over several stages he said
speaking about the recent kirkuk oil deal with the kurdistan regional government krg ghadhban said state oil marketer somo had received preferential prices albeit for low quantities
iraq last month restarted exports of kirkuk oil halted a year ago due to a standoff between the central government and the krg after a new government in baghdad agreed a tentative deal with erbil
