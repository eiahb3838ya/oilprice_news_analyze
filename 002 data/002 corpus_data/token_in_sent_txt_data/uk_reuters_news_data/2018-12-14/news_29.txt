fitch downgrades pakistan citing fiscal "deterioration"
dec reuters global credit rating agency fitch downgraded pakistan s foreign currency issuer default rating to from b citing a rise in external financing risk from low reserves and high external debt repayments along with a continued deterioration in the economy s fiscal position
pakistan s rating outlook is stable fitch ratings said on friday
inflation in the country has risen due to a significant rupee depreciation and higher energy prices fitch noted
last month pakistan and the international monetary fund failed to agree on a bailout package during a visit by an imf delegation
pakistani officials had set as the target date for the imf to sign off on the country s second bailout since when islamabad was loaned billion
the rating agency said successful talks on imf support could help in stabilising pakistan s external finances but added that the programme will face a significant implementation risk

in the same month pakistan also announced that saudi arabia had offered billion in foreign currency support to the country s central bank and a further loan worth billion in deferred payments for oil

however it also remarked that pakistan s tensions with neighbouring countries pose a geopolitical risk
