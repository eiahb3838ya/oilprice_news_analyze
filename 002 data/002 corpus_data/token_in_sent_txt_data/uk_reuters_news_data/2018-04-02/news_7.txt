u.s. epa rejects fuel efficiency standards for automobiles
washington april reuters the environmental protection agency on monday rejected an plan to make automobiles more fuel efficient in a win for car and oil companies and the latest move by the trump administration to roll back environmental regulations
epa administrator scott pruitt said that agency must revise the rules known as corporate average fuel economy or cafe standards on model year to vehicles agency spokeswoman liz bowman said
pruitt did not offer any specifics on doing so
the standards called for roughly doubling by the average fuel efficiency of new vehicles sold in the united states to about miles km per gallon and were seen by proponents as a way to spur innovation in clean technologies
