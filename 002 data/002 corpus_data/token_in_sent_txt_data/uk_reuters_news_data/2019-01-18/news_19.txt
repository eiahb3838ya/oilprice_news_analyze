tens of thousands cut off from aid in northeast nigeria after attack
dakar jan thomson reuters foundation tens of thousands of displaced people in northeast nigeria are cut off from humanitarian aid following a militant attack earlier this week the united nations said on friday
the town of rann in borno state was overrun by islamist militants on monday evening before being by the nigerian military the next day
about nigerians who had fled fighting were living in the town and dependent on aid agencies for food and health services including nutrition therapy for starving children said the office for the coordination of humanitarian affairs ocha
since the attack humanitarians have been unable to access rann by either road or helicopter with no idea when they might be able to return said ocha spokeswoman samantha newport
the attacks in rann that are increasingly frequent are having a devastating impact on the civilians taking refuge in this isolated town and severely affecting our ability to deliver aid to women men and children in need said edward kallon the humanitarian coordinator in nigeria
nigeria s northeast is home to two islamist insurgencies boko haram and islamic state west africa iswa which broke away as a faction in
the conflict is now in its year
attacks have increased during the to a february election in which president muhammadu buhari will seek a second term
the world food programme delivered food aid in the end of december to rann so people may still have enough to eat but markets and shelters were also burned down in the attack said ocha spokeswoman newport
about nigerians fled from rann across the nearby border into cameroon but the unhcr reported that almost all of them were forced to return
up to people died in rann in when nigeria s air force accidentally bombed a refugee camp saying the site was not marked on its maps
violence caused at least aid workers to pull out of the affected areas between november and january the most since the humanitarian response scaled up in ocha said adding that some have since returned
