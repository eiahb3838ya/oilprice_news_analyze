kenya imposes 16 pct vat on petroleum products, defying lawmakers
nairobi sept reuters kenyans will pay a percent value added tax on all petroleum products from saturday the revenue authority said a move that defies a parliamentary vote to delay the tax for two years
kenyan lawmakers on thursday voted to delay the tax which had already been postponed several times citing the high cost of living
that was seen as a blow to government efforts to raise revenues through higher taxes
but the revenue authority said in a statement that it was proceeding based on previously approved budget bills
kenya revenue authority informs the general public oil marketers resellers and retailers that vat will be charged on all petroleum products at a rate of percent on all transactions with effect from it said
to become law the bill on delaying the tax that was passed in parliament still needs approval by president uhuru kenyatta
analysts had said on thursday that kenya s fiscal deficit was likely to be higher than projected after parliament rejected most of the tax measures proposed by the government for the budget
kenya s legislature also rejected a robin hood tax of percent on bank transfers of over shillings and an employee contribution scheme towards the national housing development fund
the rejected tax hikes were designed to fund a range of government development goals including universal healthcare and affordable housing
