ceraweek-colombia jumps into latam oil auction wave, to award blocks in april
houston reuters colombia plans to award up to oil blocks next month and will begin routinely offering onshore and offshore areas for exploration and production in may the head of the country s energy regulator told reuters
the move colombia s first offering of new areas in four years comes as most of its neighbors also pursue new energy investments
this year amid higher crude prices and an increase in exploration capital bidding rounds are also set in argentina brazil ecuador mexico and uruguay
the upcoming colombian awards will be in the jacinto basin in the country s northwest orlando velandia president of the national hydrocarbons agency said in an interview on the sidelines of the ceraweek energy conference
colombia needs to boost foreign investment to revive its stagnant crude and gas production
the andean country plans to only slightly increase output to barrels of oil equivalent per day this year velandia said
colombia s last oilfield auction in secured investment from firms including conocophillips and parex resources for seven unconventional projects that are about to finish the exploration stage to later start early production of shale oil and gas
we want to award the maximum possible amount of blocks in this process
this pause has been useful for reflecting and gaining experience in a low price environment velandia said
parex resources and gran tierra chinese cnooc s unit nexen petroleum colombia s hocol repsol s talisman energy and noble energy qualified for the jacinto auction
they will submit offers in the coming days with results expected in early april velandia said
after completing the auction colombia expects to begin permanently offering blocks in a process similar to one brazil is planning this year
colombia s offer will include about two dozen onshore and offshore areas that can receive bids at any time
the new mechanism was approved last year and it will be tested in may
colombia s priority is to develop its offshore resources velandia said
nine permits granted to firms to study areas in the caribbean sea also plan to be converted into exploration and production contracts doubling the offshore projects in that area where big discoveries by firms including anadarko petroleum have been recently made
company ecopetrol plans to invest up to billion in largely in exploration and production
private firms in the country are expected to invest a further billion to billion
