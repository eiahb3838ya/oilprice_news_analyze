oil prices seesaw as u.s. trade dispute with china rattles market
oil prices generally supported by supply restraint soaring output undermines opec efforts to prop up prices china seeks alternative to saudi supplies by henning gloystein singapore april reuters oil prices dipped on tuesday easing after strong gains in the previous session when hopes that trade disputes between the united states and china could be resolved buoyed global markets
despite a softening of trade concerns oil markets still face an abundance of supplies that puts pressure on producers to keep their prices competitive in order not to lose market share
wti crude futures were at a barrel at gmt down cents or percent from their previous settlement
brent crude futures were at per barrel down cents or percent
the dips came after a more than percent rally on monday during european and american trade hours
oil prices rose sharply on monday as a weaker and easing concerns about the trade war saw investor appetite return anz bank said
reports that talks over the trade dispute between the and china are ongoing helped soothe investor angst it added
concerns of a prolonged trade dispute between the world s two biggest economies and uncertainty over the supply and demand balance of global oil markets have resulted in volatile yet recent trading
oil prices remain rangebound with wti oil right in the middle of the per barrel range that has largely held since january of this year said william o loughlin investment analyst at australia s rivkin securities

oil inventories had been rising for the past couple of months but the data released last week showed an unexpected draw
this week s data may be crucial for determining the direction of wti he added
the american petroleum institute is due to publish oil storage data later on tuesday while official data from the energy information administration eia is due on wednesday
oil markets have generally been supported by healthy demand as well as supply restraint led by the organization of the petroleum exporting countries opec
however soaring crude production which has jumped by a quarter since to million barrels per day bpd is threatening to undermine opec s efforts to tighten the market and prop up prices
the united states late last year overtook top exporter saudi arabia as the world s second biggest crude producer
only russia pumps more crude out of the ground at almost million bpd
in a sign that oil supplies remain ample china s sinopec asia s largest refiner plans to cut saudi crude imports in may by percent instead buying from alternative sources after saudi aramco set prices a company official said on monday
