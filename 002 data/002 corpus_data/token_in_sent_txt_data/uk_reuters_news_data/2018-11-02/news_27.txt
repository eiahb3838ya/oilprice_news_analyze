update 1-turkey has u.s. waiver on iran sanctions- minister
adds quotes istanbul nov reuters turkey has been told it will receive a waiver on sanctions against iranian oil sales turkish energy minister fatih donmez said on friday
the restoration of sanctions is part of a wider effort by president donald trump to force iran to curb its nuclear and missile programs as well as its support for proxy forces in yemen syria lebanon and other parts of the middle east
washington says it will temporarily allow eight jurisdictions to keep importing iranian oil when the sanctions come back into force on monday
just a while ago ministry officials brought the news that the secretary of state made a statement on limiting or lifting of sanctions for eight countries including turkey donmez told reporters
he said he welcomed the move as turkey had previously warned that the sanctions would negatively impact the peace and economy of neighbouring countries like turkey
nato member turkey depends heavily on imports to meet its energy needs and neighbouring iran has been one of its main sources of oil because of its proximity the quality of its crude and favourable price differentials
an industry source said last month turkey had already made efforts to cut its purchases ahead of the sanctions but would prefer to keep up some level of iranian oil imports past november
