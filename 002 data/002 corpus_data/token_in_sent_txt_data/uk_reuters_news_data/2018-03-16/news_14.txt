russia's gazprom to buy turbines from siemens for chechnya plant
moscow reuters russian gas giant gazprom said on friday it has agreed to purchase two gas turbines from germany s siemens for installation at a power plant it is building in russia s caucasus chechnya region
siemens said last year that it was reviewing aspects of its dealings with russia after claiming that four of its turbines meant for southern russia had been delivered to crimea which is subject to european sanctions on technology supplies after russia annexed the region
in october however siemens said it was ready to participate in the modernisation of power plants in russia
gazprom whose electric power arm gazprom energoholding is one of russia s biggest power suppliers plans to open a plant in the chechnen capital grozny next year with a capacity of up to megawatts
it did not say in its statement on friday how much it would pay for the two turbines
chechnya fought two wars against russia after the soviet collapse but now in return for generous subsidies and a wide degree of autonomy pledges loyalty to moscow
chechen leader ramzan kadyrov a close ally of russian president vladimir putin has been blacklisted as part of western sanctions against russia but businesses in chechnya are not subject to sanctions
