japanese trader itochu seeks north sea sale - document
london reuters japanese commodity trading house itochu is seeking buyers for stakes in two north sea oilfields a pipeline and a terminal a document seen by reuters shows
the sale run by scotiabank could fetch up to million according to banking sources
the transaction would mark the latest exit of a player in the ageing basin as a new generation of companies many with the backing of private equity seeks to inject new life into oil and gas fields
itochu through its british subsidiary cieco uk is offering to sell its percent stake in the western isles field cluster in the northern north sea located some km miles east of the shetland islands
the field which is operated by british group dana petroleum started production in november and yielded barrels of oil per day in april according to the sale brochure
itochu is also selling a percent stake in the hudson field and small stakes in the brent system pipeline and the sullom voe terminal
bids are due by midday london time on june the document showed
itochu and scotiabank did not respond to requests for comment
itochu recently agreed to buy a percent stake in the iraqi west qurna oilfield from royal dutch shell signalling that the japanese firm is turning its attention to new regions
