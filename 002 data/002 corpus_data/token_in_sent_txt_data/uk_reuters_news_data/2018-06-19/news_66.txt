oil prices dip on expectations of rising opec, russian supplies
saudi arabia russia pushing for modest output increase china trade dispute threatens american oil exports by henning gloystein singapore june reuters oil prices fell in early asian trading on tuesday on expectations that producer cartel opec and key ally russia will gradually increase output after withholding supplies since
brent crude futures the international benchmark for oil prices were at per barrel at gmt down cents or percent from their last close
west texas intermediate wti crude futures were at a barrel down cents or percent
the organization of the petroleum exporting countries opec together with a group of producers that includes russia started withholding oil supplies in to end a global glut and prop up prices
following a sharp increase in crude prices from their per barrel lows in the group on june will meet in vienna austria to discuss forward policy
greg mckenna chief market strategist at futures brokerage axitrader said there would likely be oil price volatility in the week ahead of the meeting
opec is fractured or fracturing mckenna said as iran venezuela and iraq seek to veto the production increase
we could be seeing the relationship between the saudis and russia pushing opec into second place he added
rob thummel managing director at asset management firm tortoise said he would recommend a small increase in production as the global oil market is potentially vulnerable to an oil price spike due to low inventories
we believe that opec will act like a central bank going forward raising and lowering production as necessary with an objective of keeping global oil inventories at normal levels thummel said
the other key development for global markets is the escalating trade dispute between the united states and china in which both sides have threatened stiff tariffs on each others key export goods
if implemented china may react to tariffs by putting a percent duty on crude oil imports which have been surging since to a business now worth almost billion per month
energy consultancy wood mackenzie said the united states would find it hard to find an alternative market that is as big as china
