adds quotes background by karin strohecker london oct reuters the nigerian central bank may reduce the amount it has ordered south african telecoms firm mtn to repatriate as part of an ongoing disagreement central bank governor godwin emefiele said on sunday
mtn and the central bank are in a dispute over the transfer of billion of funds which the bank said the company had sent abroad in breach of regulations
nigeria accounts for a third of mtn s annual core profit
i don t think it will be staying at billion emefiele said during a visit to london adding he expected the issue to be dealt with amicably and equitably
i want to believe that the figures will reduce
whether they will be dropped completely i honestly can not say at this emefiele said the central bank had received documents some two weeks ago from mtn and four lenders involved in the case standard chartered stanbic ibtc bank citibank and diamond bank and was in communications with all parties involved
the central bank will be examining these then it will be escalated up to my level he said adding he expected to get the results in a couple of weeks
the two sides are locked in a court dispute over the transaction
the central bank filed a on friday to a court request by mtn which is seeking to stop the bank from forcing it to bring back the money
emefiele said the mtn case was a and the central bank was not looking at transactions involving any other companies operating in nigeria
we respect the sanctity of these companies he said
shares in mtn lost almost percent over the past week
emefiele also said nigeria s central bank would continue to intervene in the foreign exchange markets adding that he believed in a stable exchange rate regime
nigeria has been battling to defend its currency and shore up its reserves of around billion hobbled by lower oil prices
