u.s. gulf coast crude imports fall to 1-year low as exports surge
houston nov reuters gulf coast crude oil imports in october fell to the lowest level in more than a year as the nation s production and exports soared data from refinitiv eikon and market intelligence firm kpler show
crude imports in the gulf coast or padd region averaged million barrels per day bpd in october down from million bpd the month before and the lowest since september refinitiv data show
booming shale oil production has triggered pipeline bottlenecks in the permian basin and bakken fields pushing domestic light sweet crude prices per barrel cheaper than the global benchmark brent
refiners have boosted their purchases in response
they can t process that much of the lighter crude but if it s cheap enough they ll figure out how to blend it in and use more of it said sandy fielden an energy analyst at research firm morningstar
