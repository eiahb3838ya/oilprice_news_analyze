australia, southeast asian summit ends with rebukes against trade protectionism
sydney reuters the leaders of australia and singapore closed a regional summit on sunday with a stand against protectionism arguing in favor of trade deals as fears mount that plans for new tariffs could stoke a global trade war
we strongly believe that a free open and multilateral trading system is key to the region s growth and prosperity singapore s prime minister lee hsien loong said at a news conference to mark the end of a summit between australia and the association of southeast asian nations asean
president donald trump announced the tariffs on march to protect domestic steel and aluminum producers on national security grounds
the united states is also looking at tariffs on up to billion worth of chinese imports targeting technology and telecommunications sectors
lee and turnbull urged asean to speedily agree to the regional comprehensive economic partnership a alternative to the partnership tpp trade pact that trump withdrew the united states from last year
