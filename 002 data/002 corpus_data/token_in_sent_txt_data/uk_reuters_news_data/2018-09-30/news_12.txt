update 1-saudi crown prince to discuss neutral zone oil output during kuwait trip - source
dubai reuters saudi arabia s crown prince mohammad bin salman is expected to discuss the resumption of oil output from the neutral zone which the kingdom s shares with kuwait during a trip to the gulf arab state on sunday a source familiar with the matter told reuters
prince mohammad will be accompanied by energy minister khalid during his trip to kuwait two separate sources said
the saudi crown prince will hold talks with kuwaiti emir sheikh sabah the kuwaiti news agency has reported
the closure of the neutral zone s jointly operated oilfields mainly khafji and wafra has become a political sticking point between the two gulf opec allies and senior officials have been trying to resolve the issue for months
it was not clear whether the renewed talks on the neutral zone would result in the resumption of oil production from the area one of the sources said
the saudi government media office did not immediately respond to a reuters request for comment
khafji was shut in october for environmental reasons and wafra has been shut since may due to operating difficulties
any restart would come at a sensitive time for the oil markets as washington presses riyadh to increase oil production to bring crude prices down
oil prices rose more than percent on friday with brent climbing to a high as sanctions on tehran squeezed iranian crude exports tightening supply even as other key exporters increased production
president donald trump called saudi arabia s king salman on saturday and they discussed efforts being made to maintain supplies to ensure oil market stability and global economic growth saudi state news agency spa reported
the call comes days after the president called again on opec to boost crude output to cool the market ahead of midterm elections in november for congress members
saudi arabia is the world s top oil exporter and opec s leader
the kingdom the only country with a significant spare capacity is worried about the tight ability of other producers to raise output amid declining iranian supplies
the resumption of the neutral zone s oilfields could add up to barrels per day of oil output capacity to both saudi arabia and kuwait
